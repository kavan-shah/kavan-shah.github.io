About me

Hi! I'm Kavan Shah, a Robotics and Controls Engineer from Ahmedabad, India, currently working in the U.S. manufacturing sector. Welcome to my website!

I'm passionate about advancing industrial automation through the integration of robotics, Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT), and cutting-edge digital technologies.

In 2023, I earned my Master's degree in Robotics from the University of Michigan. During my time there, I conducted research with the Advanced Manufacturing Lab, focusing on developing smart manufacturing solutions that blend existing infrastructure with modern technologies. My goal is to bridge the gap between traditional manufacturing processes and the future of industry, creating more efficient, flexible, and intelligent production systems. Through this website, I aim to showcase my projects, research, and the innovative solutions I've developed in the field of robotics and industrial automation.

I'm always eager to learn about new developments in the field and exchange ideas with fellow professionals, so feel free to connect with me on LinkedIn. Thank you for visiting, and I hope you find the projects and research presented here informative and inspiring.

Projects

  • Smart Manufacturing Test Bed

    Research

    The goal of this project was to develop a smart manufacturing cell with cutting-edge Industry 4.0 connectivity. The cell integrated additive (3D printers) and subtractive (CNC) manufacturing machines for part production, alongside robots for various tasks. A KUKA AMR+Cobot robot (KMR iiwa) handled part tending and delivery, while a Kawasaki SCARA robot (duAro2) managed part assembly. The system collected and monitored machine data on an IIoT platform (ADEPT), tracking machine health, cycle time, and process efficiency. Additionally, an integrated vision system provided an extra layer of safety by detecting human presence in the manufacturing environment.

    I collaborated on this project with two PhD students, Tyler Toner and Abigail Rafter. My primary responsibilities included programming the robots for their assigned tasks, establishing data communication with the IIoT software, developing the manufacturing process, and assisting with the development of the human recognition system. We also worked closely with the developers of the IIoT software to create a GUI to display data and monitor robots in the test bed.

    One of the initial project milestones was establishing communication between the duAro2 robot and ADEPT, with data visualization on a remote monitor. This task required learning the robot's proprietary programming language, programming the robot to transfer crucial data to the ADEPT server, and planning the repetitive assembly process. After many hours in the lab, we successfully collected all joint data and end effector position data from each arm, transmitted it via UDP protocol to the server running the ADEPT framework, interpreted the signal at the ADEPT end, and displayed the data with a GUI on the lab's main screen.


    Building on this achievement, we next trained the AMR robot to navigate its environment (the testbed floor) and move around the lab without colliding with obstacles. We collaborated with a KUKA engineer to set up their KMR iiwa robot in the lab. I learned how to initiate the robot, operate the Kuka Sunrise.OS software, and create a map of the lab using the LiDARs mounted on the robot's edges.


    The culmination of our efforts resulted in the successful completion of the project. Through persistent troubleshooting and refinement, we were able to integrate all components seamlessly. This allowed us to execute the entire manufacturing cycle in the testbed, demonstrating the full capabilities of our smart manufacturing cell. The final system showcased the potential of Industry 4.0 principles in a practical, functioning environment.
